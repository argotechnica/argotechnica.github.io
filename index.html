Becoming-Scientist

Hi! I’m a longtime technologist/data whisperer, now studying full-time at the University of Oxford’s Oxford Internet Institute in pursuit of a Master of Science degree in Social Science of the Internet. I also work part-time as Wikimedia Data Assistant for the Bodleian Libraries, where I use Python and SPARQL to help integrate the Bodleian’s scholarly data into Wikidata, the free and open knowledge base.

For my dissertation (currently in progress), I’m investigating emerging forms of online education in the field of data science, with particular attention to the changing role of universities in global knowledge networks.

Before Oxford, I worked full-time in IT while completing a Bachelor of Science in Multidisciplinary Studies degree at the University of Minnesota—Twin Cities. For the BSc, I combined courses in business, IT, cultural studies, and sociology to critically study Information and Communication Technologies (ICTs) in business and society. My last professional role was as System and Analytics Lead for the learning technology ecosystem of a large company. Throughout my career, I’ve managed systems, teams, projects, and organizational change; developed custom data integration tools with SQL, Python, and other technologies; and dabbled in information architecture, user experience, knowledge management, agile project management, corporate training, and coaching.

I’m seeking full-time employment after I graduate in Fall 2017. If you’d like to hire me, collaborate on a project, ask a question, or just chat, please say hello!

Somewhat experimentally, I’m also starting to write blog content at this website—check out the Posts section to see more. If you have any suggestions or requests, let me know!
