# Hello, world!

I’m a full-time MSc student in the Social Science of the Internet degree at the Oxford Internet Institute, a multidisciplinary department at the University of Oxford dedicated to understanding life online. My research interest is in how knowledge production and distribution are changing online. In my dissertation, I’m investigating emerging forms of online education in the field of data science, with particular attention to how this reflects back on the changing role of universities in global knowledge networks.

I also work part-time as Wikimedia Data Assistant for the Bodleian Libraries at Oxford, where I get to apply my skills with Python and SPARQL to help integrate the Bodleian’s scholarly data into Wikidata, the free and open knowledge base.

For undergrad, I designed a custom degree with courses in business, IT, educational psychology, cultural studies, and sociology to critically study Information and Communication Technologies (ICTs) in business and society. In my 10 years of experience in IT, I’ve managed systems, teams, projects, and organizational change, including through the development of data solutions in SQL, Python, and other tools.
